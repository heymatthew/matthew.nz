Reflections on Retros

I have a beef with Scrum Retrospectives when we create actions without owners, avoid difficult conversations, and don’t see changes over time. The format does not get outcomes for me, but it seems true for those operating at Senior levels or above.

I prefer to take proactive steps adjust the team environment. I do this by taking action and directly talking to stakeholders. That could mean include engaging delivery managers, creating Architectural Decision Records to align on a technical implementation, or promoting best practice through pairing.

But lets not endorse the heliocentric model of meetings, those earlier in their careers appreciate the structure as a way to affect change. A predictable format loans courage to share perspective that would otherwise hold back. Listen, suggest actions, take ownership of outcomes, and give gratitude.

I had a beef with Scrum Retrospectives, but now I see I needed to change how I engage with them. On reflection, teams where I had senior members who listened and lifted people up felt like better places. I can’t pay them back, so I might as well pay it forward.
