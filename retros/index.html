Reflections on Retros

Without care the Scrum Retrospective format becomes ineffective. Hallmarks include actions without owners, avoidance of difficult conversations, and productivity stagnation.

I prefer to take proactive steps adjust the team environment. I do this by taking action and directly talking to stakeholders. That could mean engaging delivery managers, creating Architectural Decision Records, or promoting best practice through pairing.

But lets not endorse the heliocentric model of meetings, those earlier in their careers appreciate structure to help support their growth. On reflection, teams where I had senior members who listened and lifted people up had great Retrospectives. You can’t pay them back, so pay it forward. Step up and show care for the process by changing your perspective.

Make a difference by listening, suggesting actions, giving gratitude, creating safe spaces for dialogue, and taking ownership of outcomes.
