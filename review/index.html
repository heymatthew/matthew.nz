Code Review

People who refactor are right, but if they make enormous pull requests they’re bad their reviewers. People never signed up for your refactors, and you’re hidinkg the changes you need to make.

People who focus on just the problem are right, but if they completely ignore the soft part of software they’re killing their codebase.

Here’s the problem, you need to be both. That’s where stacked changes help. Make the change easy then make the easy change, do it and make it clear by breaking out PRs.

  • Reorder your commits to push refactors to the bottom
  • Push 2 branches to separate rework vs the change
  • Use update-refs on rebase
  • Articulate to your reviewer how they’re related
  • Describe tradeoffs inline
