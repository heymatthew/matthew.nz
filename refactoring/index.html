Refactoring

Jeff Bezos characterised decisions as “two-way” or “one-way” doors. A two way door means you can reverse a decision, but a one way door is difficult or expensive to reverse.

Framing things in this way is freeing because you can allow yourself to experiment knowing things can be undone. Detailed planning is just not required for easily reversable decisions.

Something I’ve grown to realise is that in my workplace some of these experimental decisions have the people around me feeling like they’re irreversable.

This happens all the time. Kitset home has an original 70s kitchen and the owner is afraid to renovate. Asbestosis? A plumber can’t upgrade the hot water cyllander without fixing ventilation causing the budget to blow out. Chorus just drilled aanother hole through the wall instead of re-running the cable.

  • Refactoring
  • Holistic overview / systems thinking
  • Get robust at measuring and testing

What do I want people to walk away feeling?

That decisions about the structure of code are not permanent, that it’s healthy to experiment, we shouldn’t go into too much detail with structure unless our objects reflect the domain language lines up (see DDD).
